# Past Resolved Tickets (Reference Examples)

## Ticket #10231 - Wrong item received
Customer received a blue jacket instead of the black one ordered (order
#A7734). Resolution: issued a free replacement in the correct color with
expedited shipping at no charge, and let the customer keep the wrong item
rather than requiring a return. No return label fee applied since the error
was on our fulfillment side.

## Ticket #10298 - Delayed shipment, customer traveling
Customer needed the order to arrive before a trip and it was running 2 days
behind the standard 5-7 day window. Resolution: agent upgraded shipping to
expedited at no extra cost and proactively emailed the new estimated
delivery date. Order was reclassified urgency=medium since travel deadlines
count as a real deadline but not a safety issue.

## Ticket #10355 - Duplicate charge
Customer was charged twice for order #B1120 due to a payment gateway retry.
Resolution: the duplicate charge ($42.50) was refunded to the original
payment method within 24 hours after verifying both charges in the payment
system; this did not require manager approval since it was under the $150
limit and was a clear duplicate, not a standard refund request.

## Ticket #10412 - Password reset email not arriving
Customer said the password reset email never showed up after 3 attempts.
Resolution: confirmed the email on file had a typo (customer had signed up
with a different address than they were checking). Corrected the email on
file after identity verification, then successfully sent the reset link.

## Ticket #10467 - Refund request over policy limit
Customer requested a $620 refund for a bulk order that arrived damaged.
Resolution: this exceeded the $500 support-tool limit, so it was escalated
to the finance team per policy rather than processed directly; customer was
given a 2-business-day timeframe for the finance team's response.

## Ticket #10500 - Gift card balance confusion
Customer thought their gift card had expired because they hadn't used it in
over a year. Resolution: clarified that gift cards do not expire, and helped
the customer check their current balance on the account page.
